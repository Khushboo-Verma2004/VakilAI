Rent Increase Clause Dangerous

Issue: This clause allows unlimited rent increases with very short notice.

Law: Under Rent Control Act, most states limit annual increases to 5-10% with proper notice period (typically 30-90 days).

Explanation: This clause means your landlord can suddenly increase your rent by any amount with just 15 days warning, which could make the apartment unaffordable.

Termination Clause Illegal

Issue: Unilateral termination right for landlord only.

Law: Under Transfer of Property Act, both parties must have equal termination rights with reasonable notice (typically 30 days).

Explanation: This clause lets your landlord evict you with just 7 days notice, while you must give 30 days notice.

Security Deposit Clause Unfair

Issue: Landlord has sole discretion over deposit deductions.

Law: Security deposit deductions must be reasonable and for actual damages with proper documentation.

Explanation: This means the landlord can keep as much of your deposit as they want without proving any real damage.

Agreement Date Check

Note: Verify this date matches when you actually signed and took possession.

Explanation: The agreement date affects when your rental period starts and when notices/terms begin.

Term Duration Standard

Note: 11-month agreements are common to avoid rent control registration in some states.

Explanation: This creates a fixed-term lease that automatically ends after 11 months unless renewed.
